About

My robotics journey began in 7th grade on my school's VEX team, fueled by late nights, iteration cycles, and more Black Label Mountain Dew than I should admit. A 3rd & 5th place finish at World Championships cultivated both passion for robotics and shaped how I reason about intelligent machines. I value elegant mechanical design, robust control, and intelligence that feels intuitive.

In my work, I focus on product definition, system architecture, and validation. I translate customer and operational needs into clear technical requirements and measurable outcomes, build proof-of-concept prototypes to de-risk early ideas, shape coherent hardware and software designs, and drive validation against performance and safety criteria. Where hardware and software are tightly coupled, I also own hands-on technical execution to mature capabilities into deployable systems.

Beyond robotics, I apply systems thinking to therapeutic food development and clinical trials in Pakistan, where my family is from. I lead cross-functional teams across scientific, regulatory, and operational domains to support treatment programs for severely malnourished children.

Outside of work, I train mixed martial arts, tinker, and drink a lot of tea.

Feel free to reach out if you share any of my interests or want to collaborate on a small, meaningful project.
